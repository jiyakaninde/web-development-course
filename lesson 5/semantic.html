FACTS ABOUT DOGS

Here are some fun facts about dogs: Smell: Dogs have a sense of smell that's at least 40 times better than humans. Some dogs can even use their noses to detect medical problems. Sweating: Dogs have sweat glands in their paw pads, but they primarily cool down by panting. Nose prints: Dogs have unique nose prints, similar to human fingerprints, that can be used to identify them. Dreaming: Dogs dream like humans, and have similar sleep patterns and brain activity. Birth: Puppies are born blind and deaf, and rely on their sense of smell and heat receptors to find their mother. Color blindness: Dogs are red-green colorblind, but can see blues and yellows well. Third eyelid: Dogs have a third eyelid that protects their cornea by secreting tears. Basenjis: Basenjis are one of the only dog breeds that don't bark. Instead, they make a yodeling, whimpering, or howling sound. Swimming: Some dogs are excellent swimmers. Speed: Some dogs are fast enough to beat a cheetah.
